NYC Adventure: Sights, Bites & Skyline Nights

Group Size: 9 (including 4 seniors)

189 Ogden Avenue, Unit 2, Jersey City

Dates: July 23 & July 24

Notes: I didn't choose places for dinner for both nights and lunch for day 2. We can decide what we're in the mood at that time.

Day 1: Urban Marvels & Golden Hour Views

  • 09:30 AM

    Depart Hoboken

    Transit: PATH train from Hoboken Terminal to 33rd St (~20 min, $2.90)

    Walk: ~5 min to Hudson Yards (0.3 miles)

  • 10:00 AM

    The Vessel (Hudson Yards)

    What It Is: Futuristic honeycomb-like sculpture open for viewing and photos (interior closed for climbing; base levels accessible)

    Tip: Quick stop for group photos before the High Line walk

  • 10:15 AM

    High Line Walk

    Route: The High Line starts directly next to Hudson Yards and The Vessel. Begin the walk southbound (~15–20 min, 0.8 miles).

    Tip: Great morning lighting, benches and photo ops throughout.

  • 10:45 AM

    Little Island

    What It Is: A floating park on Pier 55 with gardens, amphitheater, and free performances

    Cost: Free

    Tip: Check the event calendar in advance for jazz nights, family shows, and surprise performances.

  • 11:30 AM

    Grand Central Terminal

    Transit: Uber from Little Island to Midtown East (~15 min, $10–15, 2.3 miles)

    Tip: Use the Whispering Gallery by the Oyster Bar and grab a photo under the main hall’s zodiac ceiling.

  • 12:00 PM

    Chrysler Building (Exterior View)

    Distance: ~5 min walk east (~0.2 miles)

    Tip: Peek inside the lobby for Art Deco murals and vintage elevators.

  • 12:30 PM

    Lunch at Ess-a-Bagel

    What to Get: Everything bagel with scallion cream cheese or whitefish salad

    Price Range: $6–$14 depending on fillings

    Why It’s Iconic: Voted NYC’s best bagel spot for decades, known for their size and chew

    Tip: Go before peak lunch to avoid long lines. Great for grab-and-go or a picnic at Bryant Park.

  • 02:00 PM

    Coffee Break / Bryant Park

    Distance: 10 min walk from Chrysler (~0.4 miles)

    Tip: Look for the Bryant Park reading corner, game tables, and bathrooms (clean and free).

  • 02:45 PM

    Magnolia Bakery (Rockefeller Center)

    What to Get: Banana pudding (signature), red velvet cupcake, or classic vanilla buttercream

    Tip: Order ahead online if possible—line moves fast but can get long in afternoons.

  • 03:30 PM

    Central Park (South Entrance)

    Transit: Walk from Magnolia (~15 min, 0.7 miles) or Uber (~5 min drive)

    Visit: Gapstow Bridge, The Mall, Bethesda Terrace (short scenic loop ~45–60 min)

    Tip: Enter at 59th St for easiest access to all three destinations in a simple loop.

  • 04:30 PM

    Levain Bakery (UWS)

    What to Get: Dark chocolate chip walnut cookie (most popular), oatmeal raisin, or double chocolate

    Tip: Ask for a warm one—one cookie is big enough to share.

  • 05:00 PM

    Return to Hoboken

    Transit: Uber (~25 min) or Subway from 72nd St to Hoboken via 1 train + PATH (may require 1 transfer)

  • 06:45 PM

    Depart Hoboken for Edge

    Transit: PATH to 33rd St + walk (~25 min total, 0.4 miles walk)

    Tip: Sunset expected at ~8:20 PM; arrive early for golden hour

  • 07:15 PM

    Edge NYC (Sunset/Night View)

    Cost: $39–44 + service fee

    Tip: Book 7:15–7:30 PM slot to enjoy sunset and skyline glow; walk the angled glass floor if you’re brave!

  • 08:30 PM

    Walk or Uber to Times Square

    Distance: ~15 min walk (~0.7 miles) or Uber (~5–7 min, $8–12)

  • 08:45 PM

    Explore Times Square at Night

    Tip: Perfect time for neon lights and energetic street scene. Hold onto bags, avoid costumed characters asking for money.

  • 09:30 PM

    Return to Hoboken or Dinner

    Transit: Uber (~20 min, $15–20) or PATH from 33rd St

Day 2: Skyline Reflections & Bridge Connections

  • 08:30 AM

    PATH to World Trade Center

    Transit: Hoboken → WTC (~20 min)

  • 09:00 AM

    9/11 Memorial & Museum

    Cost: Memorial (Free); Museum ~$30–35

    Tip: Allocate time for reflection. Museum is heavy but incredibly moving—expect silence.

  • 10:45 AM

    One World Observatory

    Distance: Next door (0.1 miles)

    Cost: ~$30–38

    Tip: Go to the Sky Portal, a glass floor that shows the street below in real time.

  • 12:00 PM

    Statue of Liberty & Ellis Island

    Transit: Walk ~10 min to Castle Clinton in Battery Park

    Ferry Cost: ~$24 adults / ~$18 seniors round-trip

    Duration: ~2.5–3 hours including ferry and time on islands

    Tip: Book pedestal access in advance. Expect 15–20 min ferry ride each way.

  • 03:00 PM

    Teo Work Call and Lunch

  • 03:30 PM

    Walk Across Brooklyn Bridge

    Transit: Walk ~10–12 min from Battery Park to bridge entrance (~0.5 miles)

    Tip: Walk on the left side, avoid bike lane. Bring water and sun protection.

  • 04:00 PM

    Brooklyn Bridge Park / DUMBO

    Distance: Short walk from bridge exit (~0.4 miles)

    Tip: Grab photos at Washington Street with bridge perfectly framed. Ice cream stands nearby.

  • 05:00 PM

    Return to Hoboken or Dinner

    Transit: Subway from Borough Hall (4/5 line) to WTC + PATH to Hoboken (~25–30 min total) or Uber (~15–20 min, $15–25)
